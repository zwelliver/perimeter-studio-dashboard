# Perimeter Studio Forecast - Intake Form Questions

Use these questions when gathering forecast/pipeline video requests to ensure accurate capacity planning and AI scoring.

---

## Required Questions

### 1. **Video Title/Name**
- *What should we call this video project?*
- Example: "2026 Holy Week Campaign Video"

### 2. **Project Description**
- *Briefly describe what this video is about and what you're hoping to communicate.*
- Example: "A 3-minute promotional video inviting the congregation to Holy Week services, featuring testimonies from members about the meaning of Easter."

### 3. **Video Type/Purpose**
- *What is the primary purpose of this video?*
  - [ ] **Equipping** - Discipleship/teaching content (teaching series, Life on Life, strategic partnerships)
  - [ ] **Enriching** - Inspiring stories and opportunities (testimonies, campaigns, seasonal content, music videos)
  - [ ] **Ministry Support** - Supporting a formal Perimeter ministry (event promos, recaps, ministry profiles)
  - [ ] **Partner Support** - Supporting a partner or informal ministry (church plants, nonprofits, global partners)

### 4. **Target Audience**
- *Who is this video primarily for?*
  - [ ] **Church-wide** - Most/all of the congregation (Sunday services, major updates, Jeff Norris content)
  - [ ] **Community** - External audience, newcomers (Plan Your Visit, public-facing promos)
  - [ ] **People Groups** - Specific demographics (men, women, youth, students, children, families)
  - [ ] **Ministry Groups** - Specific groups or partners (elders, staff, specific ministry teams)

### 5. **Content Scope/Longevity**
- *How long will this content be relevant?*
  - [ ] **Evergreen** - Long-term, reusable content (website staples, teaching series)
  - [ ] **Seasonal** - 3-6 months relevance (Holy Week, Christmas, Advent, campaign content)
  - [ ] **Event-Driven** - 1-3 months relevance (summer series, ministry event promos)
  - [ ] **Short-Term** - Single-use or <1 month (one-off events, social media reels)

### 6. **Estimated Video Duration**
- *How long do you envision the final video being?*
  - [ ] Under 1 minute
  - [ ] 1-2 minutes
  - [ ] 2-4 minutes
  - [ ] 4-8 minutes
  - [ ] 8-15 minutes
  - [ ] 15+ minutes
  - [ ] Not sure yet

**IMPORTANT:** Video duration is the PRIMARY driver of complexity and capacity allocation. Be as accurate as possible.

### 7. **Production Requirements**
- *What production elements will this video need?* (Select all that apply)
  - [ ] **Talking head** - Single person speaking to camera
  - [ ] **Interview/Testimonies** - Multiple people sharing stories
  - [ ] **B-roll footage** - Additional footage to be captured (locations, activities, events)
  - [ ] **Graphics/Text overlays** - Basic text and graphic elements
  - [ ] **Motion graphics** - Animated graphics and effects
  - [ ] **Music/Sound design** - Background music and audio mixing
  - [ ] **Multiple locations** - Filming at different sites
  - [ ] **Not sure yet** - Need consultation with video team

### 8. **B-Roll Requirements**
- *Will this video require capturing additional B-roll footage* (beyond talking heads/interviews)?
  - [ ] Yes - We need B-roll footage captured
  - [ ] No - Just interviews/talking heads
  - [ ] Not sure

**Note:** B-roll significantly increases production time and requires scheduling additional filming days.

### 9. **Estimated Timeline**
- *When would you ideally like to start working on this?*
  - Estimated Start Date: ___________

- *When do you need the final video completed?*
  - Estimated Due Date: ___________

### 10. **Related Ministry/Department**
- *Which ministry or department is this for?*
- Example: Children's Ministry, Young Adults, Pastoral Team, Communications, etc.

### 11. **Key People/Talent**
- *Who needs to be featured or involved?*
  - [ ] Jeff Norris (Senior Pastor)
  - [ ] Other staff/pastors (specify): ___________
  - [ ] Church members/volunteers (how many?): ___________
  - [ ] To be determined

### 12. **Additional Context**
- *Is there anything else we should know about this project?*
- Examples: Special requirements, dependencies, related campaigns, ministry priorities, etc.

---

## Optional Questions (Helpful for Planning)

### 13. **Confidence Level**
- *How confident are you that this project will move forward?*
  - [ ] **Confirmed** - Already approved, definitely happening
  - [ ] **Likely** - Strong probability (75%+)
  - [ ] **Possible** - Under consideration (50%)
  - [ ] **Exploratory** - Just exploring the idea

### 14. **Priority Level** (Your Perspective)
- *How important is this project to your ministry?*
  - [ ] Critical - Core ministry priority
  - [ ] High - Important but not urgent
  - [ ] Medium - Nice to have
  - [ ] Low - Can be postponed if needed

**Note:** Final priority will be determined by the AI scoring system based on organizational guidelines.

---

## How This Information is Used

1. **AI Scoring**: Your responses are sent to Grok AI which analyzes them and calculates:
   - Priority score (1-12)
   - Complexity score (1-12)
   - Category alignment
   - Estimated capacity allocation

2. **Capacity Planning**: The system automatically:
   - Creates forecast tasks in Asana
   - Adds them to capacity tracking heatmaps
   - Shows projected workload months in advance
   - Identifies potential capacity conflicts

3. **Proactive Scheduling**: The team can:
   - See what's coming down the pipeline
   - Balance current and future workload
   - Make informed decisions about project acceptance
   - Plan resource allocation strategically

---

## Submission Process

1. Submit this form with as much detail as possible
2. Video team reviews and may reach out for clarification
3. Forecast task is created in Asana with AI-generated scores
4. Task appears in Forecast capacity heatmap
5. When ready to move forward, task transitions to Preproduction phase
